Art 🎨

I am inspired by the vibrant flowers and baby animals of spring, the stillness of winter, the bold reds of the Northern Cardinal, and deep blues of the Steller’s Jay. I have been making art for several years, working mainly with Japanese watercolors and colored pencils.

It is my joy to be able to share my love of art with others. From when I was a child, hosting art lessons for my cousins at family reunions to now, hosting middle school art classes for English Language Learners as a guest teacher- I enjoy showing others the joy of creating.

I paint what I remember from distant memories blended with inspiration for the present. I reminisce about the warm Hood River sun nurturing the cherry orchards, the chill breeze and rocky coast of the PNW, the wondrous North Eastern woodlands harboring glades of Mayapples, and books I read long ago. I incorporate my feelings and energy into my art through selective chosen subjects and colors.

Most of all I enjoy creating and sitting in quiet reflection for what the universe has chosen to reveal to me.
